Q; What payments or fees should I prepare once my service entrance is approved, including bill deposit, processing fee, membership fee, or ID card cost?

Q; How much is the re-inspection fee for a disapproved service entrance, and what payment is required?
Q; What are the requirements, components, and steps for installing a new service entrance?
Q; How much is the reinspection of service entrance?
A; Here’s a complete guide for your BATELEC II service entrance.\n\nPayments and Fees\nOnce your service entrance is approved, kindly prepare the following payments:\n- Bill deposit based on your submitted load schedule\n- Processing Fee: ₱50\n- Membership ID Card: ₱50\n- Membership Fee: ₱5\n- Re-inspection Fee: only if your initial service needs a recheck\n\nHandling a Disapproved Service Entrance\nIf your service entrance is disapproved, follow the adjustments listed in the Acknowledgement of Inspection. Once fixed, call the nearest BATELEC II office to schedule a re-inspection and get your connection approved smoothly.\n\nRe-Inspection Fee\nThe re-inspection fee for a service entrance is ₱100 per re-inspection.\n\nService Entrance Components and Installation Guidelines\nYour service entrance should include the following components:\n- Line Side #8 THHN Copper or Bronze Wire\n- Entrance Cap\n- Secondary Rack\n- Load Side #8 THHN Copper or Bronze Wire\n- Rigid Steel Conduit (RSC) Pipe\n- RSC Clamp\n- Class-100 Meter Base (Jocelyn Force Brand)\n- PVC\n- Weatherproof Enclosed Circuit Breaker\n- Ground Rod\n- Copper Wire #8\n- SFT\n- Class-200 kWh Meter\n\nInstallation Tips\n- Keep the line side exposed and do not bury it in cement to prevent water or rust damage.\n- Use THHN #8 copper or bronze wire for safe electricity flow.\n- Connect the entrance to the nearest BATELEC II post not more than 30 meters away.\n- Install the meter at 5 feet from the ground for easy access.\n- Follow wire color codes: Red/Blue/Black for hotline, White for neutral, and Green for ground.\n- Use a weatherproof breaker to protect against overloads and short circuits.\n- Ensure the ground rod is securely connected for safety.
